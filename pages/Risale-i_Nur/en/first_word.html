THE FIRST WORD

“Bismillah” (In the name of Allah) is the beginning of all good. We too begin with it. Know, my soul! This blessed phrase is not only the symbol of Islam, but also the continual invocation in the language of all existence. If you want to understand what a great, inexhaustible power and endless blessing “Bismillah” is, look and listen to the following illustrative illustrative: example, analogy story:

Suppose a man traveling in the Bedouin deserts must take the name of a tribal chief and come under his protection so that he may be saved from the evildoers and meet his needs. Otherwise, he would be alone against countless enemies and would be miserable.

For such a journey, two men set out into the desert. One was humble humble: modest and the other was proud proud: arrogant, conceited . The humble one took the name of a chief, the proud one did not. The one who took it traveled safely everywhere. If he met a highwayman highwayman: bandit, robber he would say, “I travel under the name of so-and-so chief.” The evildoers would avoid him and not bother him. If he entered a tent, he would be respected because of that name. The proud one suffered so many troubles on his journey that they are indescribable. He was always trembling and always begging. He became both lowly and disgraced.

So, O proud soul! You are that traveler. This world is a desert. Your weakness and poverty are limitless.

Your enemies and needs are endless. Since this is so, take the name of the Eternal Owner Eternal Owner: Allah of this desert and the Everlasting Judge Everlasting Judge: Allah so that you may be saved from the begging of the entire universe and not tremble before any event.

Indeed, this phrase is such a blessed treasure that it binds your infinite weakness and poverty to infinite power and mercy of The All-Powerful, The Merciful The All-Powerful, The Merciful: Allah and makes your weakness and poverty the most accepted intercessor in His presence. Yes, he who acts with this phrase is like one who enlists as a soldier. He acts in the name of the state. He fears no one. He says “In the name of the law, in the name of the state” and does every job and endures everything.

As we said at the beginning: All existence, in its language, says “Bismillah.” Is that so?

Yes, just as you might see a man come and forcefully move all the city’s people somewhere and make them work involuntarily, you know well that the man is not acting by his own name or power. Rather, he is a soldier acting on behalf of the state’s power.

So too, everything acts in the name of Allah, as tiny particles like seeds carry big trees, lifting heavy loads. This means every tree says “Bismillah.” They fill their hands with the fruits from the treasure of mercy. They serve us.

Every garden says “Bismillah.” From the Kitchen of Power Kitchen of Power: The power’s source comes a pot cooking many kinds of delicious dishes together. Every blessed animal such as cows, camels, sheep, and goats say “Bismillah.” They become fountains of milk from the mercy’s bounty. They offer us food as The Provider The Provider: Allah in the most delicate and pure form, like water of life.

Every plant, tree, and grass with silk-like soft roots and veins say “Bismillah.” They pierce through hard stones and soil. They say “In the name of Allah, in the name of the Most Merciful.” Everything is made subservient to them.

Indeed, like the spreading of branches in the air and bearing fruit, the roots in hard stones and soil spread with great ease and bear fruit underground; the soft green leaves remain alive for months against the intense heat, striking nature’s face like a hard slap, poking its blind eye, saying:

Even the strongest firmness and heat you rely on moves under command; those silk-like soft veins act like Moses’ Staff (as) Moses’ Staff (as): The staff of Prophet Moses following the command فَاضْرِبْ بِعَصَاكَ الْحَجَرَ and split the stones.

And those delicate leaves, as thin as cigarette paper, act like the limbs of Abraham (as) Abraham (as): Prophet Abraham who recite the verse يَا نَارُ كُون۪ي بَرْدًا وَسَلَامًا against the burning heat.

Since everything essentially says “Bismillah,” they bring Allah’s blessings to us. We too must say “Bismillah.” We must give in the name of Allah and take in the name of Allah. Therefore, we should not accept from heedless people who do not give in the name of Allah.

Question: We give a price to those who act as dealers. What would the real owner of the goods, Allah, receive in return?

Answer: The one who does not give his property is a thief, the one who gives is a beggar. The one who gives the real essence of his property is truthful and generous and is the true owner of the dominion.
